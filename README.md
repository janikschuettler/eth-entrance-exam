# ETH Entrance Exam Resources

> A community-maintained archive of past ETH Zürich entrance exam (*Aufnahmeprüfung*) materials, including written exams and the only collection of oral exam protocols available anywhere online.

This repository is the backend for [janiks.me](https://janiks.me/), where candidates preparing for the ETH Zürich entrance exam can find past written exams, oral exam protocols, pass-rate statistics, and preparation tips.

## About this project

The ETH entrance exam is notoriously difficult, and while the ETH publishes a handful of official sample questions for the written exam, there has never been an official or public archive of oral exam protocols — candidates had no way to know what to expect going in.

I started this project to fix that. After taking the entrance exam myself in 2016, I wrote down my own oral exams together with those of other candidates and published them on this website. Every year since then, candidates have continued to send me their own write-ups after the exam and have allowed me to publish them here for future applicants. Thanks to their contributions, the archive continues to grow year after year.

## Contents

### Written Exams
- **Past exams (2001-2024)**: Mathematics I & II, Physics
- **Official ETH examples**: Sample questions from the official ETH website
- **Practice materials**: Including the new exam format introduced in 2021

### Oral Exams
- **Exam protocols (2016-2026)**: Memory protocols from former exam candidates
- **Exam types**: Both reduced and comprehensive oral exam formats
- **Multiple perspectives**: Protocols from various candidates showing different exam experiences

Disclaimer: The oral exam protocols are memory protocols from former candidates and are not official ETH Zurich documents. They should serve as orientation for the scope and depth of the examined material. Oral exams are individually developed and may vary for different candidates.

## How it's built

The website is a static site: plain HTML and CSS, no framework, no backend, no build step. The only JavaScript on the site is a single, one-time script that decodes an obfuscated email address on page load — there is no tracking, no analytics, and no other dynamic behavior.

## Disclaimer & License

Official sample exams are linked to from the ETH Zürich website directly.
